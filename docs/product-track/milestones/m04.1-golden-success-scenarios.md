### Kernel change — mixed-predicate traces (enabled projection + read-only sidecar)

`projection` and `read-only sidecar` were previously inexpressible: a
`projection.emitted` must ground its anchor on an `observed_truth.committed`
earlier in the same trace (`KernelContracts::anchor_source_is_valid` accepts only
that kind), but a replay trace was bound to a **single** predicate/profile and a
`ProjectionRead` predicate cannot emit observed truth. The replay verifier now
supports **mixed-predicate traces**: a scenario declares a per-action predicate
roster (`actions:`), and lifecycle reduction (`crates/causlane-replay/src/lifecycle.rs`)
evaluates each action against its own consequence profile. A producer
`RuntimeExecution` action commits the truth; a `ProjectionRead` reader anchors its
projection on it. The change is additive — single-predicate traces resolve the
default profile and behave identically (all prior replay/Kani/negative controls
stay green), and the cross-action anchor still grounds in the producer's real
attestation (no I-003 weakening).

**Formal-lane note (not prose-only):** the new `ProjectionRead`/mixed-predicate
capability is verified by the **replay oracle** (positive + two non-vacuity
controls). Deeper *abstract* proof-lane modeling (Alloy/P/Lean) of cross-action
anchor grounding is **S10 proof-hardening** follow-up; the fixture predicate lives
in `contracts/examples/projection_readonly.registry.yaml` and is intentionally not
wired into the production formal contour, so the `release_promote` bundle hash and
its receipts are untouched.
